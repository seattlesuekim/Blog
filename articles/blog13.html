date: 2014-02-06 title: Reading Material

Suppose you are new to the field of computer science and want to become more integrated into the culture. You already read Science, Nature, Journal of the ACS, and American Anthropologist. Where to start?

  • Slashdot has a lot of news that people talk about over beer. Don't be left out of the convo.
  • Hacker News is all about what's new in the world of security. You won't be lost when people talk about how SHA1 has been broken in just 2^52 operations, yet 98% of web apps using SSL still use SHA1.
  • Security Tube is just what it sounds like -- videos of hacking. It's fun*
  • Choose your own adventure. Google stuff you're interested in. Computer Science isn't an exclusive pseudo-high-society. You don't have to pay big money (or take old issues from your lab) for academic journals. Information is widely shared. For free.

*Don't do anything illegal.
